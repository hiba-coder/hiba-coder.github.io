June, 2025

JOT Journal - ECMFA Conference

We propose an innovative approach to the field of model-driven engineering and executable domain-specific languages. Our proposal, titled «Navigating the Trace of Executable Domain-Specific Languages through a Trace Domain Query Language», was accepted for publication in the Journal of Object Technology (JOT) and presented at the ECMFA 2025 conference, Koblenz, Germany. The paper addresses the challenge of extracting meaningful information from execution traces—files that record all changes to the system state—in order to support advanced system analysis. It introduces Trace Domain Query Language (TraceDQL), an executable language designed to enable domain experts to write queries using the domain terminology to retrieve the necessary data from the trace, without requiring strong programming expertise or deep understanding of the trace structure. TraceDQL is a generic and reusable language that can be applied to a given xDSL. In the paper, we showcase its application on two different xDSLs: the SMS xDSL from our previous study, and an Arduino xDSL. The implementation of TraceDQL is open-source and available on a public Gitlab repository, as well as permanently archived on Zenodo repository.

From an industrial perspective, this contribution offers a valuable and helpful solution. TraceDQL has been applied to an xDSL developed within the RODIC project, to allow KPI computation. It was also the subject of a dedicated workshop held during the third RODIC plenary session, aimed at introducing project members to TraceDQL and enabling them to use it effectively.

February, 2024

Modelsward Conference

My first paper was published at the international conference on Model-Based Software and Systems Engineering (MODELSWARD) in Rome, Italy. The article explores how Key Performanve Indicators (KPIs) can be defined directly at the language level, making them available for domain experts at the model level. It introduces a Simple Manufacturing System xDSL (SMS), which enables modeling and simulating industrial systems, along with an approach to evaluate their performance. A special thanks to Jean-Marie MOTTU and Erwan BOUSSE who supervied me through this work. The article is available here and the tool is accessible via this link. Our participation in this conference culminated in winning the Best Student Paper Award.

From an industrial perspective, this work represented the first software deliverable of the RODIC project: "A2.1 First Release of the Open-Source Evaluation Framework."
